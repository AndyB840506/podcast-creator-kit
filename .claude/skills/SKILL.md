## 5. Episode Model (Season 2 — EP.010+)

Each episode uses a videogame, film, song, or anime as the entry point, then distills principles
of experience, leadership, or human design.

### Standard 7-segment structure:
1. **Hook** — Emotional hook without revealing the character → reveal name → "Para el que no lo conoce..." (3–4 accessible lines about the series/game/film)
2. **El puente** — Natural transition from cultural reference to real operation
3. **Los datos** — Evidence with sources (Gallup, HBR, Deloitte, McKinsey, etc.)
4. **Mito o Realidad** — Minimum 3 rounds
5. **Variable segment** — "El Minuto de Acción", roles, consequences, or what the episode needs
6. **Cierre** — Two audiences: leaders first (3 questions), agents/supervisors after (direct message)
7. **Next episode teaser** + TM close with character's phrase

### Script format (EP.008+): anchor points
- Each block has an entry headline + fixed [REMATE]
- Data as short bullet — Andy develops organically
- Anecdotes as reminder — Andy tells in his own words
- Transitions removed — Andy decides how to connect
- TM remains intact (opening, closing, prohibitions)

### Script delivery: `.docx` with BTQ brand colors
